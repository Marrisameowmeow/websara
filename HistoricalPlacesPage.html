Location:

Fort Cornwallis 

The largest standing fort in Malaysia, built by the British in the late 18th century, featuring old cannons and historical exhibits near the Georgetown waterfront.

Location: Georgetown , Penang

Penang Peranakan Mansion 

A beautifully restored heritage home that reflects the opulent lifestyle of the Straits Chinese (Peranakan) community, complete with antiques and traditional décor.

Location: Georgetown , Penang

Eastern and Oriental Hotel

A luxurious colonial-style hotel established in 1885, famous for hosting celebrities and dignitaries, offering timeless elegance in the heart of George Town.

Location: Georgetown , Penang

Rumah Kelahiran P.Ramlee

The childhood home of Malaysia’s legendary entertainer, P. Ramlee, now a museum featuring his personal items, films, and musical legacy.

Location: Georgetown , Penang

Penang War Museum

Located on Bukit Batu Maung, this former British fort turned museum showcases Malaysia’s wartime history and WWII artifacts in a chilling, immersive environment.

Location: Bukit Batu Maung, Penang

Town Hall, Penang

A colonial-era building in Esplanade that once hosted elite social events, now used for exhibitions and official functions, showcasing classic British architecture.

Location: Georgetown , Penang


Pulau Aman

A peaceful fishing village island off the coast of Penang, known for its fresh seafood, traditional Malay lifestyle, and scenic views.

Location: Pulau Aman,Penang

Blue Mansion (Cheong Fatt Tze Mansion)

An iconic indigo-blue heritage building blending Chinese and Western architectural styles, once the home of a wealthy Chinese merchant, now a boutique hotel and museum.

Location: Georgetown , Penang

Masjid Jamek (Acheen Street Mosque)

One of the oldest mosques in Penang, built by Acehnese settlers in the early 1800s, reflecting a blend of Islamic and Southeast Asian architecture.

Location: Georgetown , Penang
